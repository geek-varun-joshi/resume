Managing Change for HRIS Migration from ADP to Dayforce

Background

The organization a mid-sized logistics and courier company operating across multiple regions in Canada, specializing in last-mile delivery solutions for e-commerce, retail, and corporate clients. The company employs over 700 staff, including warehouse operators, delivery personnel, customer support, and corporate teams. With rapid growth fueled by the e-commerce boom, the organization sought to modernize its HR operations to streamline workflows, improve employee engagement, and ensure compliance with evolving labor regulations.

Objective

To successfully migrate the company’s Human Resource Information System (HRIS) from ADP to Dayforce, ensuring minimal disruption to daily operations, improved user experience, and enhanced reporting and payroll accuracy.

Challenges

  • Resistance to Change: Employees were accustomed to the ADP system, and many expressed concerns about learning a new platform.
  • Data Migration: Transferring sensitive employee data while maintaining accuracy and confidentiality.
  • Training Needs: Ensuring all HR team members and end-users could effectively navigate the new system.
  • Timeline Constraints: The migration had to be completed within three months to align with fiscal year changes.
  • Integration: Ensuring the new system seamlessly integrated with existing tools such as accounting software and time-tracking devices.

Methodology

  • Stakeholder Engagement:
    - Conducted initial meetings with senior management to define objectives and align expectations.
    - Created a cross-functional project team involving IT, HR, and finance representatives.
  • Change Management Strategy:
    - Developed a communication plan to inform employees about the benefits of the new system.
    - Hosted town hall sessions and Q&A forums to address concerns and gather feedback.
  • Data Audit and Migration:
    - Performed a comprehensive audit of existing data in Dayforce to identify and rectify discrepancies.
    - Worked with Dayforce’s implementation team to ensure a secure and accurate data migration process.
  • Training and Support:
    - Designed role-specific training modules for HR staff, managers, and employees.
    - Organized hands-on workshops and provided user manuals and video tutorials.
    - Set up a dedicated support team to handle post-implementation queries.
  • Testing and Go-Live:
    - Conducted multiple rounds of testing to ensure functionality and resolve issues before the system launch.
    - Implemented a phased rollout, starting with smaller teams to minimize risk.

RESULT

- The migration to Dayforce was completed on schedule, with minimal disruption to daily operations.
- Payroll errors reduced by 30%, improving employee satisfaction.
- Reporting efficiency improved by 40%, enabling better decision-making for management.
- Over 90% of employees reported satisfaction with the new system in post-implementation surveys.
- The HR team’s administrative workload decreased, allowing them to focus on strategic initiatives.

LEARNING

Above all, this experience reinforced for me the importance of investing in people. When employees thrive, the business thrives — a principle that guides my approach to HR every day.
- Effective communication and transparent engagement with employees are critical to overcoming resistance to change.
- Investing in thorough data auditing and testing ensures a smoother transition and minimizes errors.
- Role-specific training and ongoing support are essential for building confidence and maximizing system adoption.
- A phased implementation approach allows for better risk management and iterative improvements.
- Cross-departmental collaboration is key to achieving successful system integration and meeting organizational goals.
